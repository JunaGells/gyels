    Types of Website

    Business Website

    These are created for companies to showcase their products or services, provide contact information, and establish an online presence. They often include features like service descriptions, customer testimonials, and a blog.

    Business Website

    E-commerce Websites

    These sites facilitate online shopping, allowing users to browse products, add items to a cart, and complete transactions. They often include features like product reviews, payment gateways, and inventory management.

    E-commerce Websites

    Portfolio Websites

    Often used by creative professionals, these sites showcase an individual's work, such as photography, design, writing, or art. They typically include galleries, case studies, and personal bios.

    Portfolio Websites

    Blogs

    These are regularly updated websites or web pages that focus on written content, articles, or personal journals. Blogs can cover various topics and often encourage reader interaction through comments and social sharing.

    Blogs

    Informational Websites

    These sites provide information on specific topics, organizations, or industries. They may include articles, reports, and resources aimed at educating the audience.

    Informational Websites

    Nonprofit Websites

    Designed for nonprofit organizations, these sites raise awareness, promote causes, and solicit donations. They often include information about the organization, events, and volunteer opportunities.

    Nonprofit Websites

    Community Forums

    These platforms facilitate discussions and interactions among users around specific interests or topics. They typically feature user-generated content, threads, and moderation tools.

    Community Forums

    Educational Websites

    These sites offer resources for learning, such as online courses, tutorials, or academic materials. They may include features like quizzes, certification options, and forums for discussion.

    Educational Websites

    Landing Pages

    Often used in marketing campaigns, landing pages focus on a specific product or offer to drive conversions. They usually have a clear call-to-action and minimal distractions to encourage user engagement.

    Landing Pages
